Welcome to the jellytoast blog — a small place to jot down what's going on
between releases.

The headline: **jellytoast now runs on macOS.** It's a proper native app —
Developer-ID signed and notarized, so it opens straight from the `.dmg` with no
Gatekeeper warnings — and it brings the things that make a Mac app feel like a
Mac app:

- the hardware **media keys**, plus Now Playing in Control Center and on the lock screen
- real **window vibrancy** behind the frosted chrome (and it honors *Reduce Transparency*)
- a native **menu bar** and a Dock transport menu
- **Notification Center** banners on track changes, and launch-at-login

Grab it from the [latest release](https://github.com/wolfgangwarehaus/jellytoast/releases/latest).
A **Mac App Store** listing is on the way, too — that one is working its way
through review.

With this, jellytoast ships for **Linux, Windows, and macOS**, with the same
features on each.

More soon.
